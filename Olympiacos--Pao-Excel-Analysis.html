Analyzing the Greek Derby with Excel

A Case Study of Olympiacos vs. Panathinaikos on May 8, 2023 to explor in depth the play styles and player performances.

Performance Analysis for the greek derby

General Information about the analysis

The aim of this project was to unravel the intricate details of the game through a meticulous examination of the data. By harnessing the power of Excel, I collected and meticulously analyzed a broad range of statistics, encompassing possession, shots on target, pass accuracy, and individual player performance metrics. 

My objective was to gain a comprehensive understanding of the teams' tactical strategies, strengths, weaknesses, and overall performance during this momentous derby.

Throughout the analysis, I sought to address fundamental questions that would shed light on the dynamics of this enthralling encounter. Compare each team’s offensive performance and find which player of Olympiacos’s had the best performance in the match on the offensive actions that he attempted, find which team's players made the most tackles during the match.

Find the top 5 players who made the most tackles during the match, Which of the midfielders that took part in the match had the greatest impact on the defensive actions attempted by their team? Fnd which team made the most successful passes during the match.
Find the top 5 players who made the most passes during the match, Find which team was more effective in the duels their players took part in during the match either in the air or on the ground and find which team gave their opponent more space before starting to press more energetically.

Finally, in this project, I skillfully harnessed the advanced functionalities of Excel to process and present the data effectively. Pivot tables, conditional formatting, charts, and graphs played pivotal roles in visualizing the information in a clear and intuitive manner. By employing these techniques, I created compelling visualizations that unveiled patterns and trends embedded within the match's statistics.

This case study analysis of the Olympiacos-Panathinaikos derby serves as a testament to my expertise in data analysis and my ability to extract meaningful insights from complex datasets. Through painstaking research and meticulous data exploration, I aimed to capture the essence of this thrilling clash. By unraveling the stories hidden within the numbers, I offer a unique perspective on the game and underscore the role of data analysis in sports.

Passing Accuracy Analysis

Based on the data, it is evident that Panathinaikos made the most successful passes during the match, with a total of 418 accurate passes, compared to Olympiacos' 317 successful passes. This information is reflected in the pie chart, which illustrates the passing comparison between the two teams.

Now, let's identify the top five players who made the most passes during the match:

1. H. Magnusson: He made an impressive 78 successful passes.
2. Z. Sarlija: He recorded 71 successful passes.
3. Ruben Perez: He contributed 69 successful passes.
4. D. Kourbelis: He made 55 successful passes.
5. Y. M'Vila: He completed 48 successful passes.

These players demonstrated their ability to distribute the ball effectively and contribute significantly to their team's passing game. The bar chart provides a visual representation of the individual performances of these players, showcasing their passing statistics in comparison to each other.

Overall, this analysis reveals Panathinaikos' superiority in terms of successful passes, with H. Magnusson, Z. Sarlija, Ruben Perez, D. Kourbelis, and Y. M'Vila emerging as the top performers in terms of passing contributions during the match.

Based on the data, Olympiacos demonstrated greater effectiveness in the duels their players participated in during the match, both in aerial battles and overall duels.

In terms of aerial battles won, Olympiacos came out on top with a total of 9 successful aerial duels, while Panathinaikos recorded 7. This indicates that Olympiacos' players had a slight advantage in winning challenges in the air.

Furthermore, when considering overall duels, Olympiacos again exhibited superiority by winning 37 duels, while Panathinaikos won 30 duels. This suggests that Olympiacos' players were more successful in winning challenges on the ground, including tackles, 50-50 challenges, and physical battles.

These statistics highlight Olympiacos' effectiveness in duels, both in the air and on the ground, showcasing their ability to assert dominance and compete successfully against their opponents.

Which team gave more space to the opponent?

based on the back zone passes

Based on the data provided, it appears that Olympiacos's team gave their opponent more space before starting to press more energetically compared to Panathinaikos. This conclusion is derived from the number of back zone passes made by each team.

According to the data, Panathinaikos made a total of 235 back zone passes, indicating a higher frequency of passes played in their defensive or deeper areas before initiating a more energetic press. On the other hand, Olympiacos made 147 back zone passes, suggesting that they allowed their opponents more space before increasing their pressing intensity.

The disparity in the number of back zone passes made by each team implies that Olympiacos exhibited a more patient approach in their pressing game, potentially allowing their opponents more time and space on the ball before applying pressure. This strategy may have been employed to disrupt the opponent's build-up play or to create opportunities for counter-pressing and regaining possession in more advantageous areas.

Overall, based on the number of back zone passes, Olympiacos's team gave their opponent more space before initiating a more energetic pressing approach compared to Panathinaikos.
